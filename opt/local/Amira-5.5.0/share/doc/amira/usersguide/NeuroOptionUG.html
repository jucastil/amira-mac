15 Neuro Option User's Guide

The Neuro Option is an Amira extension dedicated to users in the area of neuroscience. The option provides a set of modules designed to analyze images of the human (or vertebrate) brain.

The Neuro Option license enables the following modules:

  • ComputePerfusion
  • ComputeTensor
  • ComputeTensorOutOfCore
  • CreateGradientImage
  • EigenvectorToColor
  • ExtractEigenvalues
  • FiberTracking
  • TensorDisplay

This guide describes essential workflows in this field and is divided into three parts.

The first part explains how to use Amira to register data sets from multiple scans and modalities. The data is mapped to a frame of reference useful for comparing data over time or from different specimens. The second part explains the workflow of analyzing diffusion weighted image sequences obtained from an MRI scanner. In the third part Amira's capabilities to analyze another medical imaging modality, this time concerned with the measurement of blood perfusion in living tissue is described.

Convert to Talairach Coordinates

The Talairach or stereotaxic coordinate system of the human brain is used to describe the location of brain structures independent from individual differences in size and overall shape of the brain. The Convert to Talairach Coordinates tutorial explains how to use the ConvertTalairach script object to semi-automatically align the brain with the Talairach coordinate system. The module asks the user to define three landmarks:

  • Anterior commissure
  • Posterior commissure
  • Superior part of midsagittal plane

These three landmarks are then used to transform the data object into the Talairach coordinate system, where the right hemisphere has positive X values, the anterior part has positive Y values, and the superior part has positive Z values; with the anterior commissure being at the origin of the coordinate system.

Brain Mapping

In brain research it is often required to report the locations of structures or lesions in terms of a standardized reference frame. In the Brain-to-Brain mapping tutorial we want to demonstrate the steps involved in registering a data set to a reference brain. The result of the procedure is a brain that is registered with the reference and has the same resolution, voxel size, and position in space. Each of the steps in this tutorial can be adapted to a different part of the body, another modality or species, and can be easily applied to a larger number of studies.

Diffusion Tensor Imaging

Diffusion Weighted Imaging (DWI) and Diffusion Tensor Imaging (DTI) are relatively new imaging techniques that aim at identifying and visualizing structures like fiber tracts, tumors, or stroke areas in living tissue that are invisible to other image techniques. Amira provides a set of modules and scripts that let the user perform a thorough DTI analysis.

This section explains the usage of those modules by means of tutorials using clinical data. The data sets have been kindly provided by Prof. A. Brawanski and Dr. C. Doenitz, University of Regensburg, Germany. We would like to express our gratitude for their guidance regarding the design of the Amira Neuro Option as well as the use of Amira in neuroscience research.

  • Preparing data for a DTI analysis: This section includes a guide to automatically register, average, and resample image data loaded as DICOM.
  • Computing and visualizing the tensor: This section demonstrates how to compute the tensor field from the pre-processed data. Together with the tensor, the eigenvalues, the apparent diffusion coefficient, the fractional anisotropy, color encoded direction fields, and more are created.
  • Fiber tracking tutorial: In this section fibers and fiber bundles are tracked through the tensor field based on anatomical locations or label fields obtained from other modalities.

Brain Perfusion

Another type of imaging analysis provided in the Neuro Option is perfusion weighted MRI or CT image analysis. The modules provided compute the mean transit time (MTT), cerebral blood flow (CBF), and cerebral blood volume (CBV) from a user-supplied perfusion weighted time series. The time series captures the volume of the tissue for about 1 minute providing a view on the arrival, distribution, and wash-out of a blood contrast agent administered during the scan.

  • Brain perfusion tutorial: In this section a CT perfusion time series is loaded and blood flow through the patients brain is analyzed.

The algorithm provided by Amira computes the quantitative flow and volume values from the time series based on a block-circular singular value decomposition, which provides a correction for differences in arrival times of the contrast agent.
